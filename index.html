Research by IIT Delhi Finds Girls Named "Pakhi", "Prachi", "Divyanshi" Score Lower on IQ Tests

A groundbreaking (and mildly alarming) study conducted by the esteemed professors and students of IIT Delhi has revealed a surprising correlation between certain first names and below-average performance in standardized IQ tests.

The research, which spanned over 6 months and included a sample of 1,200 female students across the country, aimed to explore the impact of phonetic vibrations in names on neural synapse efficiency.

Key Findings

  • Girls named Pakhi, Prachi, and Divyanshi showed a consistent pattern of mistaking basic math for abstract art.
  • Forgot why they entered rooms (even when living alone).
  • Googled “how to boil water” and still burned it.

In a memory test, 87% of participants with these names forgot they were even part of a study. EEG scans showed "unusual brain lag" when their name was followed by "Can you do this?"

“We were shocked. One participant named Prachi took 45 minutes to spell ‘IQ’. Twice,” said Dr. Chandrakant Jha, lead researcher.

Conclusion

This study suggests that names like Pakhi, Prachi, and Divyanshi may be unintentionally linked to lower-than-average IQ scores—possibly due to the cosmic imbalance caused by overusing Instagram filters or refusing to share fries.
